Why Scoutplan?

If you're a scout leader, you probably have a list of things you want to do — planning your next outing, inviting compelling guests, making sure your youth have what they need to succeed. But then you're faced with the reality of what you have to do — sending emails, answering text messages, keeping track of who's coming to what event, maintaining your communication roster, updating calendars...it can be a lot.

If you're like many units, you're probably using a collection of different tools to get the job done: Gmail, Google calendar, Excel, SignUpGenius, text messaging...great products on their own, but a hassle to manage, share access, and keep updated.

Scoutplan is web software that's really good at those tedious tasks, freeing you to work on what people do best: delivering a great program. At a time when volunteers matter more than ever, let's make it easy to focus on what's most important. And because Scoutplan is made specifically for scouting, you can do a lot of things in one place, rather than stitching together a bunch of tools.

What about Scoutbook?

While there's a bit of overlap, Scoutbook covers different ground by focusing on advancement. Scoutplan is entirely focused on event management and communication. Together, the two make a potent combination.

How much does it cost?

Scoutplan is free for Scouts BSA packs, troops, crews, and ships. The author funds the project for his unit's use. If it's beneficial to others, so much the better!

Is it secure? Is it reliable?

Yes and yes. Scoutplan is hosted at Digital Ocean, a leading and secure cloud provider. Traffic is encrypted via TLS using certificates from Let's Encrypt. We use Honeybadger and Scout APM to monitor errors and performance. Server configuration is checked weekly through Qualys SSL Labs. As for reliability, Scoutplan was up and available 99.992% of the time in 2022, meaning it was down for less than one hour all year.
